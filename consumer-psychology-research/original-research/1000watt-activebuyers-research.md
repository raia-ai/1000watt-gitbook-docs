# 1000watt activebuyers research

**Date:** August 2022

## Introduction

The real estate market has been characterized by rapid and significant changes over the past thirty months. This period included an abrupt shutdown in Spring 2020, a quick rebound by summer, a phase of high-velocity sales and rapid appreciation, and is now experiencing a high-speed pull-back due to a historic jump in mortgage rates.

This research was conducted to understand the current mindset of **active homebuyers**—those who are currently navigating this fluid and confusing market. Understanding their thinking is crucial for real estate professionals to effectively serve and communicate with them.

## About the Survey

The survey was fielded on August 12, 2022, and included **600 adults** across the United States.

* **Target Audience:** All respondents indicated they plan to buy a home in the next six months. Real estate investors were screened out.
* **Current Housing Status:** 49% currently rent; 51% currently own.
* **Buying Timeline:**
  * "As soon as possible": 39%
  * "Within the next 90 days": 18%
  * "By the end of the year": 42%
* **Preparedness:**
  * Already working with an agent: 42%
  * Already pre-approved for a mortgage: 55%
* **Financing:** 80% plan to get a mortgage; 20% plan to buy with cash.
* **Gender:** 46% male; 54% female.

## Survey Results and Analysis

### Market Perception

Buyers generally have a good grasp of the market shift, recognizing that demand has weakened, but price reductions are not fully manifested. Only 23% thought they could "get a deal" right now.

| Q1: What is your perception of the real estate market right now?                | Answer (%) |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| The market is beginning to shift, but prices really haven’t changed             | 46.67%     |
| The market is still hot, prices are high, and sellers still have the upper hand | 29.67%     |
| The market is cooling, prices are falling, and I can get a deal                 | 23.50%     |

Current renters are slightly more pessimistic, with 32.79% believing sellers still have the upper hand, compared to 26.44% of current owners. This suggests a messaging opportunity tailored to renters.

| Market Perception                                                               | All Buyers | Current Renters Only | Current Owners Only |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------: | :------------------: | :-----------------: |
| The market is still hot, prices are high, and sellers still have the upper hand |   29.67%   |        32.79%        |        26.44%       |
| The market is beginning to shift, but prices really haven’t changed             |   46.67%   |        45.57%        |        47.80%       |
| The market is cooling, prices are falling, and I can get a deal                 |   23.50%   |        21.31%        |        25.76%       |

### Timing and Motivation to Buy

Despite market headwinds, a significant portion of active buyers are proceeding out of necessity.

| Q4: Do you think now is a good time to buy a home? | Answer (%) |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| No, but I need a home and am proceeding anyway     | 53.17%     |
| Yes                                                | 46.83%     |

Current renters showed a slight increase in pessimism, with 59.02% saying it is not a good time to buy, but they are proceeding anyway.

High rents are a major factor pushing renters toward homeownership.

| Q6: Is talk or news about a recession affecting how you think about buying a home? | Answer (%) |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Yes, this is the #1 reason why I plan to buy                                       | 43.93%     |
| Yes, it is part of why I want to buy                                               | 42.62%     |
| No, this hasn’t influenced my decision to buy                                      | 13.44%     |

Reasons for "Yes, it's a good time to buy" (Q7):

* Home prices are falling in my market already: 32.74%
* I think home prices will fall soon: 32.38%
* Buying a home is always a good investment: 31.32%

Reasons for "No, it's not a good time to buy" (Q8):

* Prices are still very high: 63.01%
* Mortgage rates have gone up, and my monthly payment will be higher: 36.05%

### Homebuyer Worries

Anxiety is high among homebuyers, driven by financial concerns and fear of the unknown. Addressing these fears through market expertise and smart negotiation strategies is key.

| Q9: What do you worry about most in buying a home? | Answer (%) |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Paying too much                                    | 44.17%     |
| Buying a home that turns out to have problems      | 28.17%     |
| Being able to get a mortgage                       | 17.67%     |
| Finding a good real estate agent                   | 9.67%      |

| Q10: Do you think your real estate agent has a good sense for the market right now? | Answer (%) |

| Yes, somewhat                                                                       | 33.20%     |

### Impact of Recession Talk

Talk of a recession is a major factor influencing buyers' thinking.

| Q11: Is talk or news about a recession affecting how you think about buying a home? | Answer (%) |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Yes, it makes me worry a little bit                                                 | 67.00%     |

### Awareness of Rate Buy-Downs

Affordability concerns make the concept of a rate buy-down highly relevant, yet awareness is low.

| Q12: Do you know what a mortgage rate “buy down” is? | Answer (%) |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Maybe, but not positive                              | 43.00%     |
| No, I have no idea what that is                      | 26.17%     |
| Yes, I know what that is                             | 30.83%     |

Despite low initial awareness, once explained, the concept is widely accepted:

| Q13: Would you consider paying a few thousands to a lender upfront in order to get a lower mortgage rate for the term of the loan? | Answer (%) |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Maybe, I might                                                                                                                     | 46.83%     |
| Yes, I would seriously consider this                                                                                               | 44.83%     |
| No, I would almost certainly not do this                                                                                           | 8.33%      |

## Conclusion

The research highlights clear areas where real estate professionals can differentiate themselves by filling information voids, easing buyer worries, and bolstering confidence. The key opportunities include:

* Targeted **rent-versus-buy** messaging.
* **Educating** buyers on the current market reality to counter the perception that sellers still have the upper hand.
* Addressing the fear of **"buying a home that turns out to have problems"** by emphasizing smart offer strategy, inspections, and repair concessions.
* Demonstrating **market mastery** to close the agent competency gap.
* Promoting and explaining the option of a **rate buy-down** to address affordability concerns.

The essence of effective marketing in this environment is saying or doing what competitors either can’t or won’t do.
